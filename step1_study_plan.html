On Sketchy days, swap FA reading time for 1–2 videos (~20–30 min each). Sketchy scenes replace a chunk of rote memorization — trust the process.
Non-negotiable rules
Do Anki every single day — not 0 days, not "I'll catch up." Missed days create a backlog avalanche.
Always read UWorld explanations in full — right and wrong answers. The explanations are a second textbook.
Annotate First Aid from UWorld. Your physical copy should be full of your own notes by Phase 3.
Don't add more resources. Pathoma, Boards & Beyond — only if you're truly stuck on a concept. Depth > breadth.
Track your UWorld % by system — weakest systems get extra FA time, not more Qs.
NBME / practice exam strategy
First practice exam: Free 120 (from USMLE.org) around Week 10 (mid-August). Establish your baseline.
Phase 3: NBME 28 or 30 every 2–3 weeks. These best predict your actual score.
Phase 4: UWSA1 and UWSA2 within the final 3 weeks — most predictive of your performance.
Stop timed assessments 3 days before the exam. Rest is performance.
Micro / Sketchy tips
Watch each Sketchy scene once actively (pause, predict, engage with the story). Don't passively replay.
Use a pre-made Sketchy Anki deck (Sketchy deck on AnkiHub or Zanki Micro) — don't make cards from scratch.
Rewatch a Sketchy scene when you're consistently missing Anki cards for that pathogen — 20 min beats re-reading the FA table.
